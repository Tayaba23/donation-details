The tragedy of Sylhet began in 1874, when the British created the province of Assam. But Assam had low revenue potential, and to make it financially viable, the Raj added to it the entirely-Bengali region of Sylhet, rich in natural gas, tea and fisheries. Through the ensuing decades, Sylhetis—both Hindu and Muslim—kept demanding a reunion with Bengal.
